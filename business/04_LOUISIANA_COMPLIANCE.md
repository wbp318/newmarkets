# Louisiana Compliance Guide — 318ecom LLC

Everything you need to stay legal with the state of Louisiana after forming your LLC.

---

## Annual Report (Required Every Year)

**What:** A one-page update confirming your LLC's info (address, members, registered agent) is current.

**When:** Due every year on the anniversary of your formation date.

**Fee:** $30 online / $25 by mail

**Where:** https://www.sos.la.gov/BusinessServices/FileBusinessDocuments/Pages/default.aspx

**What happens if you miss it:** The Secretary of State will send a notice. If you don't file within 90 days of the notice, your LLC gets **administratively revoked**. You can reinstate it, but there are late fees and it's a hassle. Set a calendar reminder.

---

## Louisiana State Income Tax

**What:** Louisiana has a state income tax. As a single-member LLC (disregarded entity), your business income passes through to your personal state return.

**Individual rates (2024):**
- 1.85% on first $12,500
- 3.50% on $12,501–$50,000
- 4.25% on income over $50,000

**File:** Louisiana Form IT-540 (Individual Income Tax Return)

**Where:** https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/

**When:** Due April 15, same as federal

---

## Louisiana Sales Tax

**Do you need it?** It depends:

- **Selling physical products to Louisiana customers** → Yes, you need a sales tax permit
- **Selling only to customers outside Louisiana (LATAM markets)** → Likely no, but register anyway to be safe
- **Selling digital products/services** → Louisiana taxes some digital goods; consult the Department of Revenue

**State rate:** 4.45%
**East Carroll Parish rate:** ~5.5%
**Combined:** ~9.95%

**Register at:** https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/

**File:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on volume. The state will tell you your filing frequency when you register.

## East Carroll Parish Occupational License

**Contact:** East Carroll Parish Police Jury
**Phone:** (318) 559-2256
**Address:** 400 First Street, Lake Providence, LA 71254

**What to ask:**
- "I'm starting an online e-commerce business from my home. Do I need a parish occupational license?"
- Some parishes require one ($25-100/year), some don't for online-only businesses
- If required, it's straightforward — fill out a form, pay the fee

## Self-Employment Tax (Federal)

As an LLC taxed as a sole proprietorship, you owe self-employment tax on net business profits:

- **Social Security:** 12.4% (on first $168,600 of net earnings for 2024)
- **Medicare:** 2.9% (no cap)
- **Total:** 15.3%

You can deduct half of the self-employment tax on your personal return.

---

## Estimated Quarterly Taxes

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in federal taxes for the year, you must pay estimated quarterly taxes or face penalties.

**Due dates:**
| Quarter          | Due Date     |
|------------------|--------------|
| Jan 1 – Mar 31   | April 15     |
| Apr 1 – May 31   | June 15      |
| Jun 1 – Aug 31   | September 15 |
| Sep 1 – Dec 31   | January 15   |

**Federal:** IRS Form 1040-ES — pay at https://www.irs.gov/payments
**Louisiana:** Pay through LaTAP — https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/

## Record Keeping

Keep all business records for at least **7 years**:

- Bank statements
- Receipts for all business expenses
- Invoices (sent and received)
- Tax returns
- Contracts and agreements
- Shipping records
- Import/export documentation
- API and subscription receipts (Anthropic, hosting, domains, etc.)
