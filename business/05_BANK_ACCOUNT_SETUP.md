# Business Bank Account Setup — 318ecom LLC

A separate business bank account is non-negotiable. Mixing personal and business money is the fastest way to lose your LLC's liability protection.

---

## What to Bring

- [ ] Government-issued photo ID (driver's license)
- [ ] EIN confirmation letter (CP 575 or online printout)
- [ ] Certified copy of Articles of Organization
- [ ] Signed Operating Agreement
- [ ] Initial deposit (varies by bank, $25-100 typical)

## Online Options (No Minimum Balance)

These are popular with small e-commerce businesses:

### Mercury (mercury.com)
- Free business checking
- No minimum balance, no monthly fees
- Good integrations (QuickBooks, Stripe)
- FDIC insured
- Virtual and physical debit cards
- Best option for an e-commerce LLC

### Relay (relayfi.com)
- Free business checking
- Multiple accounts for organizing funds (profit, taxes, operating)
- No minimums or fees

### Bluevine (bluevine.com)
- Free business checking
- Earns interest on balances
- No monthly fees

---

## What to Set Up After Opening

- [ ] **Business debit card** — for all business purchases (API subscriptions, inventory, shipping, ads)
- [ ] **Separate savings account** — set aside 25-30% of profit for taxes
- [ ] **Connect to bookkeeping software** — QuickBooks, Wave, or your preferred tool
- [ ] **Set up Stripe or PayPal Business** — connect to this bank account for receiving payments
- [ ] **Never use this account for personal expenses** — and never use personal accounts for business expenses

---

## Tax Savings Account Rule of Thumb

Every time money comes in, move **30%** to a separate savings account for taxes. This covers:
- Federal income tax (~12-22% depending on bracket)
- Self-employment tax (15.3%)
- Louisiana state income tax (1.85-4.25%)

It's better to have too much set aside than to owe the IRS in April.
