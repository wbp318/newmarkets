# 318ecom LLC — Business Formation Checklist

**Entity:** 318ecom LLC
**State:** Louisiana
**Parish:** East Carroll
**Parish Seat:** Lake Providence, LA 71254

---

## Phase 1 — Form the LLC

- [ ] **Choose and verify business name**
  - Search Louisiana Secretary of State: https://www.sos.la.gov/BusinessServices/SearchForBusinessFilings/Pages/default.aspx
  - Name must include "LLC" or "L.L.C."
  - Confirm "318ecom LLC" is available

- [ ] **File Articles of Organization with Louisiana Secretary of State**
  - File online at https://www.sos.la.gov/BusinessServices/FileBusinessDocuments/Pages/default.aspx
  - Or mail to: P.O. Box 94125, Baton Rouge, LA 70804-9125
  - Filing fee: $100 (online) or $75 (mail)
  - Initial report fee: $25 (filed simultaneously)
  - See `01_ARTICLES_OF_ORGANIZATION.md` for what to fill in

- [ ] **Create Operating Agreement**
  - Louisiana does not require one to be filed, but you absolutely need one
  - See `02_OPERATING_AGREEMENT.md`
  - Sign and keep with your records

- [ ] **Get a certified copy of your Articles**
  - Request from Secretary of State after filing is approved
  - You'll need this for the bank account

## Phase 2 — Federal Tax & Banking

- [ ] **Apply for EIN (Employer Identification Number)**
  - Free at https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
  - Apply as "Limited Liability Company"
  - Single-member: taxed as sole proprietorship by default
  - Multi-member: taxed as partnership by default
  - You can elect S-Corp taxation later if it makes sense (Form 2553)
  - Print/save the EIN confirmation letter (CP 575)
  - See `03_EIN_APPLICATION.md`

- [ ] **Open a business bank account**
  - Bring: EIN letter, Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, your driver's license
  - Keep business and personal finances completely separate
  - Options near East Carroll Parish:
    - Citizens Bank & Trust (Lake Providence)
    - Tensas State Bank
    - Online: Mercury, Relay, Bluevine (all LLC-friendly, no minimum balance)
  - Get a business debit card

- [ ] **Set up bookkeeping**
  - QuickBooks, Wave (free), or even a spreadsheet to start
  - Track every dollar in and out from day one
  - Separate categories: inventory, shipping, ads, software/subscriptions, API costs

## Phase 3 — Louisiana State Requirements

- [ ] **Register with Louisiana Department of Revenue**
  - https://revenue.louisiana.gov/
  - File online at Louisiana Taxpayer Access Point (LaTAP): https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/
  - You need this for state income tax and sales tax

- [ ] **Get a Louisiana Sales Tax Certificate (if selling to LA customers)**
  - Apply through LaTAP
  - Louisiana has a combined state + parish sales tax
  - East Carroll Parish sales tax: ~5.5% (on top of 4.45% state = ~9.95% combined)
  - If selling only to LATAM markets and not LA residents, you may not need this initially
  - Consult with a tax professional

- [ ] **File Annual Report with Secretary of State**
  - Due every year by the anniversary of your formation date
  - $30 fee (online) or $25 (mail)
  - File at: https://www.sos.la.gov/BusinessServices/FileBusinessDocuments/Pages/default.aspx
  - Missing this can administratively dissolve your LLC

- [ ] **Occupational License / Business Permit**
  - Contact East Carroll Parish Police Jury: (318) 559-2256
  - 400 First Street, Lake Providence, LA 71254
  - Ask about parish occupational license requirements for an e-commerce business
  - Some parishes require one, some don't for online-only businesses

## Phase 4 — E-Commerce Specific

- [ ] **Register a domain name**
  - Suggestions: 318ecom.com, 318ecom.co, 318ecom.io
  - Namecheap or Cloudflare Registrar (cheapest)

- [ ] **Set up a business email**
  - Google Workspace ($6/mo) or Zoho Mail (free tier)
  - e.g., you@318ecom.com

- [ ] **Payment processing**
  - Stripe or PayPal Business for receiving payments
  - Apply with your EIN and business bank info
  - For LATAM markets: research local payment processors (MercadoPago integrates with MercadoLibre)

- [ ] **Business insurance (optional but recommended)**
  - General liability insurance
  - Look into NEXT Insurance or Hiscox for small e-commerce LLCs
  - Usually $30-50/month

- [ ] **International considerations**
  - Research import/export requirements for shipping to Mexico, Colombia, Brazil, Argentina
  - Consider a customs broker if shipping physical goods internationally
  - Digital products/dropshipping may have different requirements
  - Research each country's import duties and tax thresholds

## Phase 5 — Ongoing Compliance

- [ ] **Louisiana Annual Report** — due every year on your formation anniversary ($30)
- [ ] **Federal tax return** — single-member LLC files Schedule C with your 1040; multi-member files Form 1065
- [ ] **Louisiana state income tax return** — file with LA Department of Revenue
- [ ] **Self-employment tax** — 15.3% on net earnings (Social Security + Medicare)
- [ ] **Estimated quarterly taxes** — if you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax, pay quarterly (Form 1040-ES)
  - Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- [ ] **Keep records for 7 years** — receipts, bank statements, invoices, contracts
