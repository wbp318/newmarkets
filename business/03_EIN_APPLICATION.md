# EIN Application Guide — 318ecom LLC

An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is like a Social Security number for your business. You need it for your bank account, tax filings, and any business contracts.

**It's free. Do not pay anyone for this.**

---

## Apply Online (Fastest — Takes 5 Minutes)

**URL:** https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

**Available:** Monday–Friday, 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM Central Time

---

## What the Form Will Ask

### 1. Type of Entity
Select: **Limited Liability Company (LLC)**

### 2. Number of Members
Select: **1** (or however many members you have)

### 3. State of Organization
Select: **Louisiana**

### 4. Reason for Applying
Select: **Started a new business**

### 5. Responsible Party
This is the person the IRS considers in charge. For a single-member LLC, this is you.

## After You Apply

1. **Save your EIN confirmation letter (CP 575)** — print it and save the PDF
2. Your EIN is: XX-XXXXXXX (they give it to you on screen immediately)
3. The official CP 575 letter arrives by mail in 4-6 weeks, but the online confirmation is sufficient for opening a bank account
4. **Never share your EIN publicly** — treat it like sensitive business info

---

## Tax Classification

As a single-member LLC, the IRS treats you as a **disregarded entity** (sole proprietorship) by default. This means:

- Business income goes on your personal tax return (Schedule C of Form 1040)
- You pay self-employment tax (15.3%) on net profits
- No separate corporate tax return needed

### Optional: S-Corp Election
If the business starts generating significant profit (generally $40,000+/year net), you may benefit from electing S-Corp status (Form 2553). This can reduce self-employment tax. **Talk to an accountant before doing this** — it adds complexity (payroll, W-2 for yourself, etc.).

## Next Step

Once you have your EIN, go open your business bank account. Bring:
- EIN confirmation (printed or on your phone)
- Certified copy of your Articles of Organization
- Your signed Operating Agreement
- Your driver's license
